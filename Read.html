The whole work expresses his feelings about the prosperity and decline of civilization and his appeal to respect for culture. And the yearning for a sound personality. The book was published in Taiwan in August 1995, and the mainland edition was published by Wenhui Publishing House in September 1998.

«Ordinary World»

Lu Yao (China)

"The Ordinary World" is a million-character novel created by the Chinese writer Lu Yao, which is a panoramic view of contemporary urban and rural social life in China. There are three parts in the book. First published in December 1986. The book takes China’s mid-70s to mid-1980s as a background, through complex conflicts and entanglements, centering on the two brothers Sun Shaoan and Sun Shaoping, it portrays the images of many ordinary people from all walks of life at that time; labor and love, frustration and pursuit , Pain and joy, daily life and huge social conflicts are intertwined, profoundly showing the difficult and tortuous road that ordinary people have traversed in the historical process of the great age.

«A Hundred Years of Solitude»

Garcia Márquez (Colombia)

"A Hundred Years of Solitude" is a novel created by Colombian writer García Marquez.It is his masterpiece and a masterpiece of Latin American magical realism literature. It is hailed as "a great work that reproduces the historical and social landscape of Latin America." The work describes the legendary stories of seven generations of the Buendia family, and the century-old rise and fall of Macondo, a small town on the coast of the Caribbean Sea, reflecting the changing history of Latin America over the past century. The work incorporates myths and legends, folk tales, religious allusions and other mysterious factors, cleverly blending reality and illusion, showing a magnificent world of imagination, and becoming one of the most important classic literary masterpieces of the 20th century.

«A Million Pounds»

Mark Twain (American)

"A Million Pounds" is a short story written by American writer Mark Twain, published in 1893. It tells about an adventure of Henry Adams, a poor American clerk, in London. The two rich brothers in London bet they would lend Henry an uncashable million dollar bill to see how he would end up in a month. When the one-month deadline came, Henry not only did not starve to death or was arrested, but became a rich man, and won the heart of a beautiful lady. The article uses its slightly exaggerated artistic techniques to reproduce the satire and humor in the master's novels, exposing the idea of money worship in British society in the early 20th century.

«On War»

Karl von Clausewitz (Germany)

"On War" is hailed as a classic of modern Western military theories, and has played a major role in the formation and development of modern Western military thought. It is hailed as one of the 100 books that have influenced the course of history. Clausewitz himself is therefore regarded as the originator of modern Western military theory. In the book, the author reveals the nature of war that is subordinate to politics, points out the role of human factors, especially spiritual forces, expounds the historical trend of the nature of war changing to civil war, and discusses strategy and tactics, offensive and defensive, and the purpose of war. The dialectical relationship between tactics and means puts forward theories such as concentrating superior forces to wipe out the enemy.

The Soviet Supreme Presidium also promulgated the formulation of the first, second and third levels of Suvolo in July 1942. Order of the military medal of the husband. Together with the name of its author, "Science of Victory" has received widespread attention from military theorists around the world.

«On Protracted War»

Mao Zedong (China)

"On the Protracted War" is a speech made by the proletarian revolutionary Mao Zedong at the Yan'an Anti-Japanese War Research Conference from May 26 to June 3, 1938. It is a military and political work on the policies of China's Anti-Japanese War. It is a great work. The classic military theory of Marxism-Leninism was first published in July 1938. On the basis of summing up the experience of the early days of the War of Resistance Against Japan, Mao Zedong aimed at some members of the Chinese Kuomintang's "China must die" and "China's quick victory theory", as well as the tendency of some members of the Chinese Communist Party to despise guerrilla warfare. The strategy of winning the war against Japan.
