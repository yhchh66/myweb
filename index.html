Brazilian

Barbecue

Brazilian barbecue is the national banquet of Brazil. It is said to have originated at the end of the 18th century. In their free time, Brazilian cowboys skewered meat on a long sword and grilled it on a fire. It has been passed down to this day as a Brazilian barbecue. Brazilian barbecue pays attention to the original flavor, highlighting the aroma of the meat itself, and does not put too much seasoning.

Peking

Duck

Beijing roast duck was a court dish in ancient times. It is said that Taizu Zhu Yuanzhang ate a roast duck every day. Roast duck also reflects the food characteristics of our country's "not tired of eating fine and fine food". The high-quality Peking duck is slowly roasted on charcoal fire with high-quality fruits. The roasted Peking duck is slowly cut into more than 100 slices with a knife. The selection of materials, heat, and knife skills...all the traditional Chinese dietary wisdom is gathered here. Japanese

Sushi

As an island country, Japan is rich in seafood resources. It is said that when Japanese fishermen sell sashimi, it is not easy to identify the type of fish cut into pieces, so the fish skin will be tied to the sashimi with bamboo sticks to make it easier for everyone to identify. Over time, the name "sashimi" has been retained, referring to sashimi or other seafood, eaten directly with wasabi and Japanese soy sauce. Sushi is made with rice soaked in vinegar, topped with sashimi, and hand-made by the chef.
